Rules

Format

  • The puzzlehunt starts on {% format_time start_time '%A, %B %d, %Y at %I:%M %p %Z' %} and ends on {% format_time end_time '%A, %B %d, %Y at %I:%M %p %Z' %}, at which point puzzle solutions will be available. However, we will leave the website running until {% format_time close_time '%A, %B %d, %Y at %I:%M %p %Z' %} so teams can still submit answers and go through the hunt at their own pace (see the FAQ for a more detailed explanation).
  • Teams have a maximum size of 5. You may not create multiple accounts or be on multiple teams.
  • The leaderboard ranks teams by number of puzzles solved and tie-breaks by earliest last solve time.

Solving

  • Each answer is a string of letters A-Z. Lowercase letters will be changed into uppercase, and any other characters will be stripped before checking for correctness.
  • You have 10 total guesses for each puzzle. If you legitimately run out of guesses and need more, feel free to contact us, though we won’t grant your request if we see that you were, for example, guessing random thematic answers, or brute-forcing all 10-letter words.

Hints

  • If you need a hint, you can message one of your friendly Ditch Day seniors!

Other

  • You may use any external sources for help, including other people, and the Internet. We love a good collab!
  • You may not publicly stream a solve for the puzzle games during Ditch Day.
  • It won’t be necessary to look at the HTML source/Javascript/CSS on any webpages for any puzzle, but you’re welcome to look anyway.
  • We reserve the right to disqualify any team for unsportsmanlike conduct at the Virtual Olympics!
  • We reserve the right to change any of these rules. If there’s a big change, we’ll announce it to all teams.
  • If you have any questions about these rules, or if you want to contact us for any reason, email your friendly senior. We will get back to you as soon as we can.
